Check out these cool spots in Raleigh, NC.

Rumored location of a civil war era cave. You can see an embankment behind these buildings. A rail road runs on top of the embankment and ruins of an old bridge are visible nearby.

This Italianate vernacular house was built in 1881 by contractor and hardware dealer Thomas H. Briggs for his son Fabius. The house and estate were known as "Oakdale." The house became a brothel in the 1940's and later a Baptist Church. It is now occupied as a single-family home.
